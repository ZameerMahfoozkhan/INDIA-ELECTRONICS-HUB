Common Reasons Why Your TV Won't Turn On

  • Power Supply Board (SMPS) Failure: The SMPS converts mains voltage to the voltages needed by the TV. Capacitor failure, burnt MOSFETs, or damaged transformers can cause complete power failure. We repair and replace faulty SMPS components.
  • Blown Fuse: A power surge can blow the main fuse in your TV. While this seems simple, the underlying cause must be identified to prevent recurrence. We diagnose and fix the root cause.
  • Capacitor Failure: Swollen or leaking capacitors on the power supply or mainboard are a common cause of TVs not turning on. We replace failed capacitors with high-quality replacements.
  • Mainboard Issues: The main processor board controls the TV's startup sequence. Firmware corruption or component failure can prevent the TV from booting. We perform mainboard repairs and firmware updates.
  • Standby Mode Issues: TV stuck in standby with a blinking or solid red light often indicates power supply or mainboard communication problems. Our technicians diagnose the specific failure point.
  • Power Surge Damage: Lightning strikes or voltage fluctuations can damage multiple components. We assess and repair all surge-related damage to restore your TV.
  • Faulty Power Button/IR Sensor: Sometimes the issue is as simple as a faulty power button or IR receiver. We test and replace these components as needed.

Our Diagnostic Approach

When your TV won't turn on, our technician follows a systematic diagnostic process:

  • Visual Inspection: Checking for visible damage like swollen capacitors or burnt components
  • Power Supply Testing: Measuring output voltages from the SMPS to identify failures
  • Mainboard Check: Testing the mainboard's startup sequence and processor response
  • Component Testing: Individual testing of suspect components like capacitors, MOSFETs, and diodes
  • Repair & Testing: Replacing failed components and thorough testing before handover

Quick Tips Before Calling

Before calling for repair, try these simple steps:

  • Check if the power outlet is working by plugging in another device
  • Try a different power cable if available
  • Unplug the TV for 5 minutes, then plug it back in (power reset)
  • Check if the remote batteries are working — try the power button on the TV itself
